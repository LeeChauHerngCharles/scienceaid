Atoms

Atoms are made up of 3 subatomic particles. These particles are called protons, neutrons and electrons. Protons and neutrons are packed tightly in the center of the the atom, also known as the nucleus. The electrons of an atom orbit the nucleus.

Protons

Protons have a relative charge of +1 and their relative mass is 1. The number of protons in a atom defines the element.

Neutrons

Neutrons have a relative charge of 0 and their relative mass is 1. The sum of the number of protons and neutrons is represented by the nucleon number, or more commonly known as the relative atomic mass.

Electrons

Electrons have a relatice charge of -1 and their relative mass is 1/1840. As all atoms are electrically neutral, the number of electrons is similar to the number of protons.
