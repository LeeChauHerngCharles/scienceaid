Metallic Bonding and its Properties

When metals lose their electrons, the electrons form a sea of electrons. As such, the metal ions are attracted to the negatively charged sea of electrons. The electrons are delocalised and can move about.

Electrical Conductivity

Metals are good conductors of electricity as the sea of delocalised electrons can conduct electricity.

Malleability and ductility

Metals are malleable and ductile as the metal ions can slide over one another withour disrupting the metallic bonding. This is as the sea of electrons are able to move freely in the metal and will not limit the movement of the metal ions.
