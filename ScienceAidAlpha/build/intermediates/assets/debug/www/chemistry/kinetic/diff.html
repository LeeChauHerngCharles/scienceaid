Diffusion

Diffusion is the nett movement of particle from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration.

In Gas

Particles in the gaseous state move randomly at high speeds. As such, the particles in the gaseous state will be able to fill in the empty space in between the air particles. This is known as diffusion.

In Liquids

Particles in the liquid state move slower than those in the gaseous state, as such, the rate of diffusion in the liquid state is much slower than that of the gaseous state.

Effect of Temperature on the Rate of Diffusion

The rate of diffusion increases as the temperature increases. This is as the higher the temperature, the more kinetic energy the particles have. The particles move faster and this increases the rate of diffusion.

Effect of Molecular Mass on the Rate of Diffusion

The rate of diffusion increases as the molecular mass of particles decreases. This is as the particles are lighter and can move faster.
