Specialised Cells, Tissues, Organs and Systems

There are many different types of cells in living organsisms. These cells differ in size and shape and they perform specific functions.

Differentiation

It is the process by which a cell becomes specialised for a specific function.

Tissue

A tissue is a group of similar cells which work together to perform a specific function. Cells of the same type form a simple tissue. Tissues containing different types of cells are called complex tissues

Simple tissue in animals - Examples

The epithelial tissue, or the epithelium, is a sheet of cells which covers both the internal and external surfaces of the body. The sheet of cells covering the external surface of the body is called the epidermis. The muscle tissue is a collection of muscle cells which helps your body to move by contracting.

Simple tissues in plants - Examples

The leaves and stems have covering tissues called the epidermis.

Complex tissues in animals - Examples

The tissue found inside an organ or between organs is called connective tissue. It consists of a few types of cells. However the whole tissue has one function: to connect the different parts of an organ or organs together. Other examples of tissues in animals are nervous tissue, glandular tissue and bone tissue.

Complex tissues in plants - Examples

An example of complex plant tissue is vascular tissue, made up of xylem tissue and phloem tissue.

Organs

An organ is a structure made up of different tissues working together to perform a specific function.

Organs in animals - Examples

The stomach is an organ whose function is to temporarily store food, digest and churn it.

Organs in plants - Examples

A leaf is an organ for phtotsynthesis. It contains palisade tissue, spongy mesophyll tissue, xylem tissue and phloem tissue.

Organ systems

Several organs working together make up an organ system, such as the digestive system and the respiratory system. Various system work together to make up the entire organism.

Classification

Biologists group living organisms according to their similarities and differences. This is called classification.
