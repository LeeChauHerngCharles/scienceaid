Water

Water is an essential component of all body tissues. About 70% of our body wight is water. Hence, water is extremely important to life.

Functions: Solvent for chemical reasctions

Water is the solvent for many chemical reactions taking place in living things. Plants need water for photosynthesis.

Functions: Key component of tissues

Water is the key component in cells, tissue fluid, digestive juices and blood.

Functions: Controlling body temperature

Water is a component of sweat. When water in the sweat evaporates from the skin surface, latent heat is removed. The body is then cooled down.

Functions: Transport of dissolved substances

Water is a solvent which helps to transport dissolved substances such as digested products from the small intestine to other parts of the body and waste products from the cells for removal from the body.

Amount of water needed

Water is lost from the body in exhaled air, in the urine, the faeces and sweating. To replace the amount of water lost daily, a normal, healthy adult in a temperate climate requires about three litres of water a day. It depends on the following factors.

How active a person is

How healthy a person is

The environmental conditions
