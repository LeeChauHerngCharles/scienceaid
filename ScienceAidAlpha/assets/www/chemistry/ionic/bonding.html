Ionic Bonding

Ionic Bonding is the loss or gain of an electron by an atom. When a anion(non-metal) gains a electron from a cation(metal), a ionic bond is formed.
Definition: Ionic bonds are the strong electrostatic forces of attraction between positive and negative ions.

Naming conventions

A compound made from 2 elements usually has a name which ends with a 'ide'
Examples: Sodium Chloride / Zinc Oxide / Carbon Dioxide

A compond that contains hydroxide is named a hydroxide.
Examples: Potassium Hydroxide
