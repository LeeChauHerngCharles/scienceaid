Changes of State

Melting

When a substance changes from a solid to a liquid, the process is called melting. As the particles are heated, it gains kinetic energy. The particles move faster and are able overcome the forces of attraction. The particles are then able to slide over one another.

Freezing

When a substance changes from a liquid to a solid, the process is called freezing. As the particles are cooled, it loses kinetic energy. The particles move slower and are unable overcome the forces of attraction. The particles then go closer to each other until they are arranged in a orderly manner.

Boiling

When a substance changes from a liquid to a gas, the process is called boiling. As the particles are heated, it gains kinetic energy. The particles move faster and are able to overcome the forces of attraction. The particles move faster and are spread apart.

Evaporation

Evaporation is the process of a liquid state turning into a gas. Evaporation happens at any temperature. Evaporation only happens to the surface of the liquid.

Condensation

When a substance changes from a gas to a liquid, the process is called condenstaion. As the particles cool down, it loses kinetic energy. The particles move slower and are unable to overcome the forces of attraction. The particles get closer to each other until they are packed closely to one another.

Sublimation

Sublimation occurs when particles in a solid have enough kinetic energy to overcome the forces of attraction and escape as a gas.
